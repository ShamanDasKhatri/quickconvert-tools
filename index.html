How to Convert MM to Inches

To convert millimeters to inches, divide the millimeter value by 25.4. This is because one inch is exactly equal to 25.4 millimeters — a standard defined by the international yard and pound agreement of 1959.

Formula: Inches = Millimeters ÷ 25.4

For example, 50 mm ÷ 25.4 = 1.9685 inches. This converter is used in engineering drawings, woodworking, screen sizes, and international shipping where metric and imperial units must be mixed.

How to Convert Centimeters to Inches

To convert centimeters to inches, divide the centimeter value by 2.54. One inch is defined as exactly 2.54 centimeters.

Formula: Inches = Centimeters ÷ 2.54

This conversion is widely used for human height measurements. For example, a person who is 170 cm tall is 66.93 inches or 5 feet 7 inches tall. Centimeters are used in most countries while the United States still commonly uses inches and feet for height.

What is BMI and How is it Calculated?

BMI stands for Body Mass Index. It is a number calculated from your weight and height that gives a general indication of whether your weight falls within a healthy range for your height.

Formula: BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height (m)²

For example, if you weigh 70 kg and are 1.75 m tall: BMI = 70 ÷ (1.75 × 1.75) = 70 ÷ 3.0625 = 22.9. This falls in the normal weight range.

How Many Calories Do You Need Per Day?

Your daily calorie needs depend on your age, weight, height, gender and activity level. This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation — the most accurate formula recommended by dietitians for estimating daily calorie needs.

To lose weight eat 500 fewer calories than your maintenance calories. To gain weight eat 500 more. For most adults maintenance calories range from 1,600 to 3,000 calories per day.

How many calories should I eat to lose weight?
Eat 500 fewer calories than your maintenance calories to lose about 0.5 kg (1 lb) per week. Never go below 1,200 calories for women or 1,500 for men without medical supervision.
What is the Mifflin-St Jeor equation?
It is the most accurate formula for calculating Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR). For men: BMR = 10×weight + 6.25×height – 5×age + 5. For women: BMR = 10×weight + 6.25×height – 5×age – 161.

How Much Water Should You Drink Per Day?

The amount of water you need daily depends on your body weight and how active you are. A general rule used by nutritionists is to drink 30 to 40 ml of water per kilogram of body weight per day.

How to Calculate Percentages

A percentage is a number expressed as a fraction of 100. To find X% of Y, multiply Y by X and divide by 100. For example, 20% of 150 = (150 × 20) ÷ 100 = 30.

Percentages are used in everyday life for calculating discounts, tax, tips, exam scores, interest rates and much more.

How do I calculate a percentage of a number?
Multiply the number by the percentage and divide by 100. Example: 15% of 200 = (200 × 15) ÷ 100 = 30.

Divide the first number by the second and multiply by 100. Example: 30 is what % of 150? = (30 ÷ 150) × 100 = 20%.

How to Calculate a Discount Price

To find the final price after a discount, multiply the original price by the discount percentage and subtract from the original price.

Formula: Final Price = Original Price × (1 – Discount% ÷ 100)

Example: A $200 item with 25% off: 200 × (1 – 0.25) = 200 × 0.75 = $150 final price. You save $50.

How to Convert Celsius to Fahrenheit

To convert Celsius to Fahrenheit, multiply by 9/5 and add 32.

Formula: °F = (°C × 9/5) + 32

To convert Fahrenheit back to Celsius: subtract 32 then multiply by 5/9. For example, 25°C = (25 × 1.8) + 32 = 45 + 32 = 77°F. The USA uses Fahrenheit while most countries use Celsius.
